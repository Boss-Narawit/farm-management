Sawatdee, I am Lung Prasert. I've spent 25 years working the red dirt here in Nakhon Ratchasima, and managing 300 rai across 10 plots is no easy task even for an old hand like me. After looking through your app, here is my honest take from the driver's seat of my tractor.

1. WHAT IS USEFUL
The employee wage tracking is very helpful. Finding reliable labor in Korat is getting harder, and with 10 plots, I often lose track of who worked where and for how long. Being able to see the cost per rai for land prep and contractor fees is also good because it helps me see which of my plots are eating up my cash before the harvest even starts. In my experience, if you don't watch the daily labor costs, you'll have nothing left when the mill pays you.

2. CRITICAL GAPS
The biggest problem is that this app only tracks money going out, but nothing coming in. A farmer needs to see his yield and his revenue. There is no place to record the sugarcane variety—like LK92 or Khon Kaen 3—which is important because some varieties need more water or different fertilizer. Also, there is no way to track soil quality or rainfall, which are the two things that actually decide if my crop will be good or bad.

3. FERTILIZER
Fertilizer is my second biggest expense after labor, so this is a huge gap. Simply having a list of prices isn't enough. I need to know exactly when we threw the fertilizer on Plot 4 and Plot 7. A useful fertilizer log would show the date, the plot name, the formula used (like 16-8-8 or 46-0-0), and how many bags were applied. If I miss the 30, 60, or 90-day application windows, my cane won't have enough "sweetness" or weight, and I lose money at the mill.

4. HARVEST AND QUOTA
I absolutely must track harvest tonnage. Right now, I keep track of this by collecting the paper receipts from the truck drivers when they return from the mill and writing it down in my notebook. If the app doesn't track how many tons I've delivered against my mill quota, I won't know if I'm meeting my contract. Managing the quota is how I decide which plots to cut first to make sure the mill keeps taking my cane throughout the season.

5. RATOON TRACKING
Knowing the ratoon age is vital. Plant cane (the first year) always gives the best yield, but by the 3rd ratoon (the 4th harvest), the yield usually drops so low that it's not worth the fertilizer. I need to know which plots are on their 3rd cut so I can plan to plow them up and replant new seed cane in October. Without this tracking, I might waste money fertilizing a plot that should have been replanted.

6. TOP 5 FEATURE RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Fertilizer Application Log: This would let me track my 30-60-90 day schedule to ensure maximum growth and CCS levels. It helps me verify that my workers actually put the bags on the field as planned.

2. Harvest Tonnage & CCS Tracker: I need to record the weight and sugar content (CCS) from every truckload delivered to the mill. This is the only way to know which plots are actually profitable and which ones have poor soil.

3. Quota Management Dashboard: This would show me my total contracted tonnage versus what has already been delivered. It allows me to manage my harvesting schedule and truck rentals so I don't get penalized by the mill.

4. Ratoon Cycle Tracker: I need to label each plot as Plant Cane, Ratoon 1, Ratoon 2, or Ratoon 3. This tells me exactly when a plot's life cycle is ending so I can budget for the high cost of new planting and land prep.

5. Profit and Loss Summary per Plot: The app should subtract all my labor, fertilizer, and contractor costs from my harvest revenue for each plot. At the end of the day, I need to know if I actually made a profit on those 300 rai or if I'm just working for the sake of working.
